# Welcome Sequence Template

**Purpose**: Turn new subscribers into engaged users/customers
**Length**: 5 emails over 10 days
**Tone**: Warm, helpful, relationship-building

## Sequence Structure

### Email 1: Immediate Welcome (Send immediately)
**Goal**: Confirm subscription, set expectations, deliver promised value

**Framework:**
1. Thank them for joining
2. Deliver the lead magnet/promised value immediately
3. Tell them what to expect (email frequency, content types)
4. One soft CTA (explore, connect, follow)

**Psychological Angle**: Reciprocity (deliver value immediately)

---

### Email 2: Origin Story (Day 2)
**Goal**: Build connection, establish authority, humanize the brand

**Framework:**
1. Why this product/service exists
2. The problem you discovered
3. The mission or transformation you're committed to
4. Invite them into the journey

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- "Why I built [product]..."
- "The story you need to hear"
- Curiosity + personal angle

**Psychological Angle**: Liking (shared values, relatability)

---

### Email 3: Quick Win (Day 4)
**Goal**: Drive first meaningful action, demonstrate value

**Framework:**
1. One actionable tip or strategy
2. Why it works (keep it brief)
3. How to implement (specific steps)
4. Encourage reply with results

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- "Try this today: [specific tactic]"
- "Your first win with [product/service]"
- Action-oriented, outcome-focused

**Psychological Angle**: Commitment (small action creates buy-in)

---

### Email 4: Social Proof (Day 7)
**Goal**: Build trust, address skepticism, show proof

**Framework:**
1. Customer success story or testimonial
2. Specific results (numbers, before/after)
3. Relatability (someone like them)
4. CTA: "See more stories" or "Join them"

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- "How [Name] achieved [specific result]"
- "[Result] in [timeframe]: here's how"
- Specificity > vagueness

**Psychological Angle**: Social proof (others like them succeeded)

---

### Email 5: Next Steps (Day 10)
**Goal**: Convert to customer or deepen engagement

**Framework:**
1. Recap the journey so far
2. Present the next logical step
3. Address the most common objection
4. Limited-time incentive (if appropriate)
5. Clear, specific CTA

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- "Ready for the next step, {{first_name}}?"
- "Your [benefit] is waiting..."
- Assumptive, momentum-driven

**Psychological Angle**: Consistency (they've engaged this far, natural to continue)

## Tone Guidelines

### Casual:
- Contractions (we're, you'll, don't)
- Emojis sparingly (1-2 per email max)
- Conversational asides ("Here's the thing...")
- Short sentences

### Professional:
- Complete sentences, minimal contractions
- No emojis in body (acceptable in subject)
- Polished but not stiff
- Industry-appropriate language

### Friendly:
- Warm openings ("Hope your week is off to a great start")
- Encouraging language
- Use of "we" and "together"
- Personal sign-offs

### Urgent:
- Time-sensitive language
- Shorter paragraphs
- More frequent CTAs
- Emphasize opportunity cost

---

## Best Practices

**Do:**
- Welcome them by name
- Deliver value in every email
- Build progressively (don't pitch hard in email 1)
- Make it easy to reply
- Use "you" more than "we"

**Don't:**
- Apologize for emailing
- Oversell in the welcome email
- Use the same CTA five times
- Forget to deliver what you promised
- Make it all about you

---

## Timing Notes

- Email 1: Immediately (within 1 minute of signup)
- Email 2: 48 hours later (gives them time to engage with email 1)
- Email 3: 2 days after email 2 (Day 4 total)
- Email 4: 3 days after email 3 (Day 7 total)
- Email 5: 3 days after email 4 (Day 10 total)

Adjust for weekends: avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon sends when possible.
