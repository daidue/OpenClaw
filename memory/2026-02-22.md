### Proposed Two-Sided Signal Framework
**Thesis:** Divergence between stated preferences (social) and revealed preferences (trades) = alpha

**Initial proposal:**
- **Stream A (Social):** X/Twitter, Reddit sentiment tracking
- **Stream B (Behavioral):** Sleeper trade data, ADP, start/sit rates
- **Divergence patterns:** Hype fade, panic buy, silent accumulation, trust gap

**Critical analysis revealed fatal flaws:**
1. **Sample size too small** - Elite managers (5% of population) = 4-5 trades/player/week (statistically meaningless)
2. **Causation unclear** - Does social CAUSE value changes or just react to same underlying event?
3. **Circular reasoning** - Training on our own predictions amplifies errors
4. **Self-fulfilling prophecy** - 10K users see "SELL" signal → flood market → we created the crash we predicted
5. **Championship correlation = noise** - 0.7% "win rate" basically random, positional bias makes it worthless
6. **Manager quality ≠ trade skill** - Fantasy is 40% luck, championships don't predict future trade success

### Better Alternatives (Adopted)

**Outcome-Based Trade Timing** (Priority 1):
```
Track every trade: Player A for Player B
2/4/8 weeks later: Calculate which side gained value
Aggregate: "Teams acquiring Player X underperformed by -3.7% after 4 weeks"
Signal: When buying Player X, you typically overpay
```

**League-Specific Calibration with Hierarchical Fallback**:
- **Tier 1:** Global Sleeper market (50K+ public leagues)
- **Tier 2:** League cohort adjustment (similar settings)
- **Tier 3:** Your league override (15+ trades minimum)
- **Handles edge cases:** New leagues, low-activity leagues, private leagues

**Key insight:** Sleeper username connection gives access to ALL user leagues (public + private), not just public leagues

### What We Killed
❌ Manager quality weighting (sample size, causation, complexity problems)
