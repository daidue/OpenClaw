## Deep-Dive #1: LEAGUE INTELLIGENCE HUB (Score: 92/100)

### 1. Concept Summary

**League Intelligence Hub** is TitleRun's flagship feature: users import their Sleeper dynasty league and instantly receive team power rankings, roster grades, positional strengths/weaknesses, and — crucially — **personalized, mutual-benefit trade recommendations tailored to their specific league.** It transforms TitleRun from a generic trade calculator into a personalized dynasty co-pilot that knows your league, your roster, and your league-mates' rosters.

Users love it because it answers the #1 question in dynasty: **"What should I do next?"** — with specific, actionable answers.

### 2. User Story

```
As a dynasty manager, I want to sync my Sleeper league and instantly see my team grade,
my league's power rankings, and specific trades I should propose to specific league-mates
So that I can make smarter roster decisions without spending hours analyzing every roster
Because right now I have to manually browse rosters, guess what others need, and hope my
offers don't get laughed at — it takes 2+ hours to find one good trade opportunity.
```

### 3. Competitive Advantage

**What we can do that others can't:**
- Our **10-source Bayesian valuation model** provides more accurate player values than KTC's crowdsourced model or FantasyCalc's single-source approach
- Our **mutual benefit algorithm** doesn't just find "fair" trades — it finds trades where BOTH sides improve. No competitor has this.
- **Roster-context awareness** — we analyze both rosters to find complementary needs (you're deep at WR, they're deep at RB → natural trade partners)

**Why competitors can't copy easily:**
- **KTC:** Crowdsourced values, no proprietary model. They'd need to build a 10-source pipeline from scratch (12+ months).
- **FantasyCalc:** Just launched AI trade analyzer using LLM wrapper (generic, expensive, slow). Our purpose-built model is faster, cheaper, more accurate.
- **Sleeper:** They're a platform, not an analytics company. Building deep analytics isn't their core competency and they've shown no interest.
- **PlayerProfiler/PFF:** They have data but no trade engine, no mutual benefit algorithm, no league integration.

**The compounding moat:** Every league that syncs with TitleRun creates data we use to improve recommendations. As we learn which trades get accepted vs. rejected, our trade suggestion quality improves. Competitors would need both the algorithm AND the user base.

### 5. Technical Approach

**Core Algorithm:**

```
1. LEAGUE SYNC
   - User authenticates with Sleeper username (no OAuth needed — Sleeper API is public)
   - Fetch: league settings, rosters, players, draft picks, transactions
   - Store in TitleRun DB (league_id, user_rosters, settings)

2. ROSTER ANALYSIS (per team)
   - For each roster, calculate:
     a. Positional strength scores (using our Bayesian values)
     b. Team total value
     c. Age-weighted value (dynasty window: "competing" vs "rebuilding")
     d. Positional depth (starters vs bench quality)
     e. Draft capital inventory
   - Generate team grade (A+ to F) and overall power ranking

3. TRADE RECOMMENDATION ENGINE
   - For the user's team, identify:
     a. Positional weaknesses (below-average starters)
     b. Positional surplus (above-average bench/depth)
   - For each league-mate's team, identify:
     a. Complementary needs (they're weak where you're strong)
     b. Complementary surplus (they're strong where you're weak)
   - Generate candidate trades using mutual benefit algorithm:
     a. You send surplus → their weakness
     b. They send surplus → your weakness
     c. Both teams improve by our valuation model
   - Rank trades by: mutual benefit score × feasibility

4. PRESENTATION
   - League power rankings (1-12 with grades)
   - Your team: grade + strengths + weaknesses + strategy recommendation
   - Trade recommendations: top 3-5 trades with:
     - Who to trade with
     - What to send / receive
     - Why it works for both sides
     - Fairness score
     - Mutual benefit score
```

## Deep-Dive #2: BUY LOW / SELL HIGH ENGINE (Score: 85/100)

### 1. Concept Summary

The **Buy Low / Sell High Engine** tracks player valuations across our 10 Bayesian sources over time, detects momentum shifts (rising/falling consensus), and alerts users to buy/sell windows BEFORE the broader market catches up. Think of it as "insider trading" for dynasty FF — you see the signal before KTC's crowdsourced values adjust.

Users love it because it gives them a **timing edge** — the difference between buying Puka Nacua at his floor vs. chasing him at his ceiling.

### 2. User Story

```
As a dynasty manager, I want to see which players are rising or falling in value
across multiple expert sources before the market price adjusts
So that I can buy low on ascending players and sell high on declining ones
Because right now I only see KTC values (lagging, crowd-driven) and I'm always
late to value shifts — by the time I notice, the window has closed.
```

### 3. Competitive Advantage

- **Nobody has 10-source consensus momentum.** KTC is one source (crowd). FantasyCalc is one source. Dynasty Nerds is one source. We aggregate TEN and detect when they're all moving in the same direction.
- **Predictive, not reactive.** KTC values follow crowd sentiment. Our multi-source momentum LEADS crowd sentiment by 1-3 weeks.
- **Impossible to replicate quickly.** Building a 10-source Bayesian pipeline takes 12+ months.

### 4. Data Sources

| Data | Source | Cost |
|------|--------|------|
| Player valuations (10 sources) | Our existing Bayesian model pipeline | FREE (already built) |
| Historical valuation snapshots | Need to start storing weekly snapshots | FREE (our own data) |
| Market events (injuries, trades, depth chart) | NFLverse, PFR | FREE |

### 5. Technical Approach

```
1. SNAPSHOT PIPELINE (weekly during offseason, daily during season)
   - Capture current valuation from each of our 10 sources
   - Store as time-series: {player_id, source, value, date}

2. MOMENTUM CALCULATION
   - For each player, calculate:
     a. 1-week delta (% change across sources)
     b. 4-week trend (direction + acceleration)
     c. Source agreement score (how many sources trending same direction)
     d. Consensus momentum = weighted average of directional agreement

3. SIGNAL GENERATION
   - BUY signal: momentum > threshold AND current value < 90-day average
   - SELL signal: momentum < -threshold AND current value > 90-day average
   - Confidence: based on source agreement (all 10 agree = high confidence)

4. ALERT DELIVERY
   - Dashboard: "Buy Low / Sell High" page with current signals
   - Push: weekly email/notification digest of new signals
```
