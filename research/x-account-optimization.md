## 1. Profile Optimization

### Bio (160 chars)
- **Who you are** + **what you tweet about** (2-3 topics) + **credibility hook**
- Include 1-2 searchable keywords (e.g., "AI agents", "Notion", "productivity")
- Add a CTA link (Linktree, website, newsletter signup)
- Example: `Building AI agents for Notion productivity | Creator of ClawdBot | Tips on AI tools, automation & templates`

### Avatar
- High-contrast, recognizable at small sizes
- For personal brands: well-lit headshot, no sunglasses, face visible
- For product brands: clean logo that reads at 48px

### Banner/Header (1500x500)
- Reinforce your niche visually â€” tagline, product screenshots, or speaking photo
- Include brand colors and a value proposition
- Update quarterly

### Pinned Tweet
- **THE deciding factor** for profile-to-follow conversion
- Best options: high-performing thread, intro thread ("who I am"), or case study with real numbers
- Rotate every 30-60 days
- Should showcase your best work and give immediate value

### Other
- Keep account **public** (private accounts don't grow)
- Fill in location, website, and birthday fields for algorithm trust signals
- Use a consistent display name (real name or brand name, not handles)

## 2. Posting Frequency & Timing

### Volume
- **4-10 tweets per day** (including replies and quote tweets)
- Accounts posting consistently see **2.5x more impressions**
- Quality > quantity, but volume matters â€” perfectionism kills growth

### Timing (US/general audience)
- **Peak hours:** 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM (audience's local time)

- Weekends: lower competition = potential for organic reach on evergreen content

## 4. Hashtag Strategy

### 2025-2026 Reality
- Hashtags are **less important** than they were pre-2023
- The algorithm now relies more on **content relevance and engagement** than hashtag discovery
- Use **1-2 relevant hashtags max** â€” more looks spammy
- Better in bio than in every tweet
- Useful hashtags for our niche: `#AI`, `#AIagents`, `#Notion`, `#Productivity`, `#BuildInPublic`, `#IndieHacker`

### Alternative Discovery
- X's search engine indexes tweet text â€” use natural keywords instead of hashtags
- The "For You" algorithm surfaces content based on topic clusters, not hashtags

## 5. Following Strategy for Building Legitimacy

### Who to Follow (for a new account)
1. **Peers** â€” accounts at similar size building similar things (mutual engagement)
2. **Aspirational accounts** â€” bigger creators in your niche (reply to them regularly)
3. **Industry accounts** â€” official brand accounts (Anthropic, Notion, etc.)
4. **Curators/newsletters** â€” they might share your content
5. **Active engagers** â€” people who reply/retweet a lot in your niche

### Following Ratio
- Keep follow/follower ratio reasonable â€” don't follow 5,000 with 50 followers
- Early stage: follow 100-300 highly relevant accounts
- Unfollow non-engagers after 30 days if needed
- Goal: build a **curated feed** that keeps you in the conversation

### Engagement-First Following
- Don't just follow â€” **engage with their content** before and after following
- Like, reply, quote tweet their best posts
- Build genuine relationships, not transactional follows

## 6. Algorithm Tips (2025-2026)

### What the Algorithm Rewards
1. **Bookmarks** â€” strongest signal; a bookmarked tweet = high-quality content
2. **Replies** â€” especially long, threaded conversations
3. **Engagement velocity** â€” first-hour performance is critical
4. **Dwell time** â€” how long people pause on your tweet (images, threads, long-form)
5. **Consistency** â€” posting daily builds topic authority
6. **Media** â€” tweets with images/video get 2-3x more engagement
7. **Profile completeness** â€” filled-out profiles get algorithmic trust

### What the Algorithm Penalizes
- External links in tweets (add links in replies instead)
- Excessive hashtags (>3)
- Engagement bait without substance
- Inconsistent posting (going dormant then flooding)
- Buying followers/engagement (destroys reach)

### Pro Tips
- **Put links in the first reply**, not the main tweet â€” avoids link penalty
- Use **alt text on images** â€” boosts accessibility AND search discovery
- **Thread your own tweets** to keep conversations going on your posts
- Write like you talk â€” avoid corporate jargon
- Use the **80/20 rule**: 80% value/entertainment, 20% self-promotion
- **Visual tweets** (screenshots, charts, carousels) significantly outperform text-only
- Ask questions to drive replies (algorithm sees this as conversation)
